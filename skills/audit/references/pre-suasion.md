# Pre-Suasion — Reference Card

> Source: Robert Cialdini, _Pre-Suasion: A Revolutionary Way to Influence and Persuade_ (Simon & Schuster, 2016). Framework cited descriptively under nominative fair use.

## Definition

Pre-Suasion is the practice of channeling attention BEFORE the persuasion attempt so the audience's mind is primed toward concepts that favor the request. The persuasion attempt does not start with the request itself; it starts with what comes immediately before.

Cialdini's central claim: **what you do in the moment before the ask matters more than the ask itself.** The 7 principles of influence determine HOW you ask. Pre-Suasion determines what mental state the audience is in when you ask.

## Key concepts

### The privileged moment

A privileged moment is a window of focused attention, engineered by the persuader, during which the audience's mind is primed toward concepts favorable to the upcoming request.

Privileged moments are created by:

- An opener that channels attention to a specific concept (safety, belonging, scarcity, growth, loss avoidance, etc.)
- Environmental cues (image, color, setting, music, sensory context)
- Questions that direct the audience's reflection toward a particular memory, emotion, or value

The opener is the lever. Whatever the opener channels attention toward, the audience will give weight to in the moments that follow.

### Attentional channeling

The mechanism behind Pre-Suasion. Whatever the audience is currently focused on disproportionately influences their interpretation of what comes next. This is why the first sentence, the subject line, the hero image, the opening question matter more than their length suggests.

Examples of effective channeling:

- Software pitch with an opener about recent breaches → primes audience toward security thinking → security claims in the body land harder
- Coaching offer with an opener about the buyer's stated 2026 goal → primes audience toward goal achievement → the offer reads as a means to that goal, not as an unrelated purchase
- Donation appeal with an opener about a specific person (not a statistic) → primes audience toward identification → the donation ask lands harder

### Misdirection of attention (anti-pattern)

The opposite of Pre-Suasion: openers that channel attention to the SENDER, the BRAND, or to topics IRRELEVANT to the request. These openers waste the privileged moment.

Examples:

- "Welcome to Acme Corp" (channels attention to the brand, not the audience's mental state)
- "I noticed your company is in the SaaS space" (signals templated outreach; channels attention to the sender's lazy research, not the prospect's situation)
- Hero copy about company history when the request is about the buyer's future

## Audit signals

### YES — strong Pre-Suasion opener

- Opens with a concept directly relevant to the audience's existing focus or stated priority
- Channels attention toward a mental state that favors the request (e.g., loss aversion before a guarantee, growth thinking before a scaling offer, belonging before a community pitch)
- The first 1-3 sentences create a privileged moment that the rest of the copy exploits

### WEAK — opener is present but channels attention loosely

- Opener gestures at the audience but doesn't tightly couple to the request
- Opener establishes context but doesn't prime a specific mental state
- The privileged moment exists but the rest of the copy doesn't take advantage of it

### NO — opener channels attention to the wrong concept

- Opener is about the sender, the brand, or company history
- Opener triggers a mental state that contradicts the request (e.g., scarcity-of-time framing right before asking for a long demo)
- Opener is generic ("Hi [First Name], hope you're doing well") and creates no privileged moment

## When Pre-Suasion matters most

Pre-Suasion has the highest leverage on:

- Cold outreach (the recipient has zero context; the opener IS the persuasion attempt)
- Subject lines and email previews (the privileged moment is 5-10 words)
- Landing page heroes (the first 3 lines determine whether the page gets read)
- Pitch openers (the speaker has 30-60 seconds before the audience commits attention)
- Donation appeals (the framing of the cause shapes the size of the gift)

Pre-Suasion has lower leverage on:

- Documentation and reference content (the reader arrives with intent)
- Transactional emails (receipt, password reset — no persuasion attempt)
- Long-form essays where the reader has already self-selected for the topic

## Cross-reference to the 7 principles

Pre-Suasion AMPLIFIES the principles. The opener can prime any of the 7:

- Channel attention to a peer making a choice → primes for Social Proof
- Channel attention to an expert's recent statement → primes for Authority
- Channel attention to a deadline or limited supply → primes for Scarcity
- Channel attention to a shared identity marker → primes for Unity
- Channel attention to a previous commitment the reader made → primes for Consistency
- Channel attention to something the writer is giving the reader → primes for Reciprocity
- Channel attention to a warm, human moment → primes for Liking

The audit always checks BOTH: are the principles present in the body? AND is the opener priming the audience to receive them?
